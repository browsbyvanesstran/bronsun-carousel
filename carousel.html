Chart Rule · Light / Ivory

#6 Brown is your anchor shade for Light/Ivory warm skin with dark brown hair. #7 Dark Brown is not listed here — but the chart's pro tips reference it as a depth agent when more intensity is needed.

Our client is medium-full density — that confirms 3% developer. Fine or sparse brows should always drop to 1.8% to prevent over-saturation on less hair.

The Mistake Most Artists Make

Using 3% on fine brows because they want more color payoff. You'll get color — and you'll get it too dark, too fast, with no control over timing.

Same formula — #6 Brown + #7 Dark Brown, 1:1. The lamination already opened the cuticle, so 1.8% is enough to drive color in. 3% post-lamination risks going too dark too fast with no recovery.

Check at 8 minutes. Medium-full brows absorb faster than you expect once the cuticle is open. Don't walk away and come back at 12.
